THE WHAT

The Akilaah Mobile App was designed to empower cooperative members by providing a comprehensive yet user-friendly platform for managing their financial activities. From savings and loans to contributions and subscriptions, the app offered seamless access to cooperative services on the go, enabling members to track, manage, and grow their financial well-being.

This app was built to complement Akilaah’s web solution, not simply replicate it, but enhance the experience by leveraging the unique strengths of mobile technology, such as biometric security and personalized notifications.

THE WHY

While Akilaah’s web platform was effective for desktop users, it lacked the accessibility and immediacy that today’s mobile-first audience demands. Cooperative members expressed the need for:

  • Convenience: A mobile solution for quick access to financial tools without being tethered to a desktop.
  • Efficiency: Intuitive design that streamlined complex processes like loan tracking and contribution management.
  • Security: Stronger safeguards for handling sensitive financial transactions on mobile devices.

By addressing these needs, Akilaah aimed to expand its reach and engagement, particularly among younger, mobile-savvy users, while maintaining the trust of its traditional user base.

THE HOW

Research and Discovery

The foundation of this project was understanding user pain points and opportunities to enhance the cooperative experience.

  • Stakeholder Interviews: Conducted sessions with cooperative managers and members to understand workflows, challenges, and priorities.
  • User Surveys: Gathered data from 100+ participants, revealing key preferences for mobile usage, such as simplified interfaces and real-time updates.
  • Competitive Analysis: Evaluated similar financial and cooperative apps to identify gaps and best practices.

By addressing these needs, Akilaah aimed to expand its reach and engagement, particularly among younger, mobile-savvy users, while maintaining the trust of its traditional user base.

KEY INSIGHTS

85% of users preferred mobile access over desktop due to convenience.

Members highlighted the need for clear navigation, real-time updates, and visual feedback on financial trends.

Younger members (aged 25–40) expressed a preference for modern, minimalist designs, while older users valued straightforward functionality.

DESIGN PROCESS

Audit & Ideation

  • Audited Akilaah’s existing web platform to identify workflows that could be reimagined for mobile.
  • Proposed mobile-first solutions to reduce friction and improve engagement, such as consolidating related features into unified modules.

Wireframing And User Flows

  • Created wireframes focusing on key tasks like loan requests, savings creation, and subscription management.
  • Designed user flows to minimize steps required for high-priority actions, ensuring a smoother experience.

Prototyping and Testing

  • Built interactive prototypes using Figma for iterative testing.
  • Conducted usability tests with cooperative members to validate designs, leading to changes such as larger touch targets and more prominent notifications for pending actions.

UI Design

  • Developed a clean, modern interface incorporating Akilaah’s brand identity.
  • Focused on accessibility with clear typography, color contrast, and intuitive iconography.

KEY TAKEAWAYS

  1. Empathy Drives Design: Deep engagement with agents, members, and cooperative managers ensured the portal addressed real needs.
  2. Simplification Is Key: Focusing on intuitive workflows and minimal complexity made the portal accessible to users with varying tech skills.
  3. Feedback Matters: Iterative testing allowed for refinements that significantly improved usability and adoption.
  4. Transparency Builds Trust: Providing real-time updates and accurate financial tracking fostered trust among all stakeholders.
