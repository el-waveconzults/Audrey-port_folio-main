Dashboard Wireframe

Before starting the dashboard design process, I sketched
several wire frames as part of my ideation process.
However, as I worked closely with my client, Credence Bank, the
wire frames evolved to align with their specific needs and
business goals. These modifications were made to ensure that
the client’s requirements were met while also guaranteeing an intuitive
user experience for end-users. Below is the wireframe that serves as the foundation for the final dashboard UI design.

UI Components

Cards

These cards are interactive, allowing users to click or hover over them for more detailed insights or to drill down into specific data points.

Quick Action Tab

The "Quick Action" is a dedicated area that provides users with easy and immediate access to important actions or tasks related to financial management their financial status, including their account.

Analytics Bar Chart

An analytics bar chart is a graphical representation used to display and visualize
data in the form of horizontal or vertical bars. Bar charts are commonly used in
analytics to show comparisons between different categories or data points.

Navigation Bar

The side navigation bar contains links or icons that represent different sections, pages, or
features of the web application. Users can click on these links to navigate to specific content or
perform actions.

Transactions Tab

The Transactions tab allows users to review and manage their financial history, track expenses,
reconcile accounts to gain insights into their financial activities. It's especially useful for individuals,
businesses, and financial professionals who need a detailed view of their financial transactions.

Other Screens

Sign In Screen

A Sign-in screen is a fundamental element of user authentication in websites and applications.
Its primary purpose is to allow users to securely access their accounts by entering their credentials.
